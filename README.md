# what:
a project to 'algorithmize' abstract-expressionist painting processes, and to study the outcomes that may emerge. 

---

# on abstract expressionism:
tldr: a broad painting movement where abstract forms are drawn for emotional-expression. since the form is abstract, painters exploit the medium to communicate said emotion (paint, application, et-cetera). 

more resources:
- [wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abstract_expressionism)
- [moca-video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pe4xm2XwSEA) 
- [britannica](https://www.britannica.com/art/Abstract-Expressionism)

# why: 
when i first looked at videos of [jackson-pollock painting](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X3Uj_HAAvbk), i couldn't help but draw connections to [generative art](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generative_art). while abstract-expressionism is usually characterized as a human act of expression, i found pollock's paintings to be largely algorithmic & pseudorandom; thereby capable of being replicated by an algorithm. 

therefore, in studying more of these painters & 'algorithmizing' their processes, i attempt to explore what the role of a human was, and whether a piece of algorithmic work can (mistakenly, or not) be interpreted as a piece of emotionally expressive work.
